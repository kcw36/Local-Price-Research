## P3 — Programmatic Quote Requests

**What:** Programmatically submit quote requests to tradespeople who don't publish prices, then aggregate and analyse the responses.

**Why:** The core data premise — that tradespeople publish prices on their websites — is weaker than expected. Manual checks show most UK tradespeople quote on request. This feature would bypass that limitation by requesting quotes directly.

**Pros:** Would dramatically increase price data availability; quotes are actual market prices rather than listed rates; could surface pricing patterns that website scraping can't.

**Cons:** High complexity — requires email sending, response parsing (LLM), and handling diverse reply formats. Ethical/legal questions around automated contact. Delays of days or weeks for responses. Rate-limit risk from email providers.

**Context:** Only worth building if the scraping approach (Yell + Checkatrade + website visits) consistently returns <5 businesses with published prices per search. Validate data volume first. If validated, approach: use a generic enquiry template ("I'm researching local rates — could you give me a rough ballpark for a standard callout + hourly rate?"), send via SendGrid, parse responses with LLM, store in SQLite alongside scraped prices.

**Effort:** L (human: ~2 weeks / CC: ~1hr)
**Depends on:** Scraping working well enough to identify which businesses have no published prices; email infrastructure (SendGrid); confirmed that the contact info is accurate enough to reach real humans
